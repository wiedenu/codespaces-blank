FY27 Marketing Scorecard · 30,000-ft view

The scorecard on one page

Heather's five FY27 priorities, each reduced to the one thing we'd watch to know it's working. A benchmark year: we baseline the signals now and set targets later.

Framework: Goals (the priority) → Signals (how we'd know) → Measures (the number on the dashboard).

The five priorities — and the one thing we watch for each

1

Align Marketing & Sales on a lead-gen strategy that drives measurable pipeline

Watch: qualified leads (SAL) + marketing-sourced pipeline contribution. Success signal: inbound requests trending down (alignment is working).

2

Run focused, multi-channel programs that generate qualified leads and cut low-value work

Watch: qualified lead volume by campaign + channel performance. Success signal: low-value / redundant activity down (JOMO).

3

Improve the martech stack and data quality for lead gen, reporting, and compliance

Watch: data quality + system-integration health (deliverability, clean data, end-to-end lead tracking).

4

Strengthen brand awareness and positioning to support lead gen and engagement

Watch: qualified web traffic (SEO / AEO / GEO) + brand visibility and thought-leadership reach.

5

Build an engaged, high-performing team that operates with autonomy, focus, and alignment

Watch: team engagement + ownership of strategy, role clarity, focus across workload.

It all rolls up to a few headline numbers

These are what leadership actually judges Marketing on:

Qualified leads (SAL)
The headline lead-gen number
Lead → opportunity conversion
Are the leads good, not just plentiful
Marketing-influenced pipeline
The revenue tie

Brand visibility / qualified traffic feeds the top of that funnel; data + integration health is the enabler underneath it all. Two of the three headline numbers (conversion, pipeline) unlock the moment the Salesforce / Activity Sync plumbing lands.

Benchmark year

Baseline the signals now; set directional targets once the baselines are trustworthy.

No vanity metrics

Raw sessions, pageviews, and opens stay context, never KPIs. If it can rise while nothing improves, it isn't a KPI.

No new projects

The measures come from work the team is already doing. This names how we'll know it's working.
