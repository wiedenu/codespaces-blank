HubSpot AI / Breeze Assistant

What it is, what it's not — Marketing Team Reference
We're enabling Breeze Assistant first — HubSpot's conversational AI helper. We are not enabling Breeze Agents (autonomous, multi-step AI) at this time. Same idea as Microsoft Copilot: it drafts, you review, you decide.

What's Being Enabled vs. What's Not

Breeze Assistant — enabling now Conversational, human-in-the-loop. You ask, it drafts, you review and edit before anything goes live.
Breeze Agents — not enabled Autonomous, multi-step AI that executes campaigns/content on its own. Not part of this rollout.

What You Can Use It For

  • Email subject line and body copy variations
  • First-draft blog post outlines or sections
  • Ad copy variations for paid social/search
  • Landing page copy drafts
  • SEO meta description / title tag suggestions
  • Summarizing a contact or company record before a call
  • Drafting follow-up replies based on context you provide

What It Can't / Shouldn't Be Used For

  • No auto-publish — every draft needs human review before it goes live
  • Doesn't know our brand voice or naming standards automatically — only knows what you put in the prompt; anything drafted still goes through our existing brand/naming governance
  • No autonomous customer-facing responses — nothing replies to a customer or dealer unsupervised; anything touching rates, terms, or commitments goes through normal compliance review regardless of how good the draft looks
  • No persistent memory — it doesn't learn your preferences over time; each interaction starts fresh
  • Not a strategy or judgment tool — it accelerates drafting, not decision-making

Common Questions

Will it write and publish content for me?
No. Drafts only — you review and publish.
Will this replace my job?
No. It removes the blank-page problem on first drafts. Judgment and final review are still yours.
Can I use it to reply to a customer directly?
Not unsupervised. Run anything customer-facing through normal review, especially anything touching rates or terms.
Does it already know our brand voice?
Only what you tell it. Feed it context — don't assume it knows GreatAmerica's standards.
