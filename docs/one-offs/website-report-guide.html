GreatAmerica Website Performance Dashboard

Director's Guide  ·  How to read and use the report

This dashboard shows how GreatAmerica's websites — greatamerica.com, portfolioservicesgroup.com, and irhcapital.com — are performing across the full marketing funnel: from how often we appear in search results, to how many visitors engage with the site, to how many become new contacts in HubSpot. Data is pulled directly from Google Analytics 4, Google Search Console, and HubSpot.

Controls — How to Navigate the Report

Date Range
Buttons at the top switch between Last 7 Days, 28 Days, 30 Days, 90 Days, and Last 12 Months. All metrics and charts update instantly. Start with Last 30 Days for a current snapshot, then switch to Last 12 Months for trend context.
Site Filter
All Sites shows aggregate performance across all three properties. Select GreatAmerica, PSG, or IRH Capital to isolate a single site. Useful when one site is driving a spike or dip in the combined view.
View Toggle
Flow (default) shows metrics as a left-to-right progression with charts. Funnel shows the same data as a visual funnel — useful for quickly spotting where volume drops off between stages.

Reading the Funnel — What Each Metric Means

The report is organized as a marketing funnel in two stages: Discovery (search visibility) and Engagement & Conversion (site behavior and lead capture).

GSC Impressions
How often we show up in search
The number of times a GreatAmerica site appeared in a Google search result. Does not require a click — just appearing counts.
Watch for: Sustained drops may signal a rankings issue or content gap.
GSC Organic Clicks
How many people clicked through
The number of times someone clicked a search result and landed on our site. This is the organic traffic Google Search drives to us.
Watch for: Clicks declining while impressions hold = lower CTR, titles/descriptions may need work.
GSC Click-Through Rate
% of searchers who clicked us
Clicks divided by impressions. A 2–3% CTR is typical for most B2B sites. Higher is better — it means our search listings are compelling.
Watch for: CTR below 2% suggests our page titles and descriptions aren't winning clicks.
GSC Avg Position
Where we rank in search results
Average ranking position across all queries we appeared for. Lower numbers are better — position 1 is the top result. Positions 1–10 are page one.
Watch for: Position trending above 20 means most of our traffic comes from page 2+.
GA4 Sessions
Total visits to our sites
Every visit to the site, from any source — organic search, direct, email, paid ads, referrals. This is the top-line traffic number.
Watch for: Session counts significantly higher than organic clicks means non-search channels (email, direct) are driving volume.
GA4 Engaged Sessions
Visits with meaningful activity
Sessions where a visitor stayed 10+ seconds, viewed 2+ pages, or completed a key action. The engagement rate (engaged ÷ sessions) reflects content relevance.
Watch for: Engagement rate below 35% suggests traffic isn't finding what it needs.
HubSpot Form Submissions
Visitors who raised their hand
Completed form submissions from tracked GreatAmerica forms — contact requests, content downloads, event registrations, giveaways, and more.
Watch for: Low form submissions relative to sessions = visitors aren't converting; check CTAs and offer relevance.
HubSpot New Contacts
Net-new people in our database
New contacts created in HubSpot with a first-touch URL matching a GreatAmerica site. This is the bottom-line lead generation number from the web.
Watch for: New contacts significantly lower than form submissions = re-submits from existing contacts (not necessarily bad).

The Charts

Search Visibility
Impressions (blue area) and Clicks (purple line) over time. Impressions on the left axis, Clicks on the right. Look for the gap between them — a widening gap means our ranking is holding but fewer people are clicking through.
Site Engagement
Sessions (green), Engaged Sessions (teal), and Form Submissions (orange) over time. Sessions and engaged sessions should trend together. Spikes in form submissions often align with email sends or campaign launches.
Where Leads Came From
A horizontal bar chart showing new contacts by original traffic source. Direct and Organic Search are typically the top two. Email Marketing reflects contacts driven by campaigns. Paid Search reflects Google Ads activity.

Note on the 90-day and 12-month views: At those ranges, the charts automatically roll up to weekly and monthly buckets to keep the visuals readable. The metric totals at the top always reflect the full period — only the chart granularity changes.

Questions This Report Answers

Is our search visibility growing or declining?
Compare impressions and clicks across date ranges. Last 12 Months shows the year-over-year trend. Filter to GreatAmerica or IRH Capital to isolate a specific site.
How many new leads did our websites generate this month?
Set the range to Last 30 Days. The New Contacts card is the answer. Switch between sites to see which property is contributing most.
Are people actually engaging when they visit, or bouncing?
The engagement rate (shown below Engaged Sessions) tells you. A rate above 40% is healthy. Below 35% warrants a look at page content and load times.
Which channel is driving the most leads?
The "Where Leads Came From" chart at the bottom shows new contacts by original source. Organic Search, Direct, Email, and Paid Search are the typical top contributors.
How is PSG or IRH Capital performing compared to the main site?
Use the Site selector to switch between properties. All metrics and charts update to reflect only that site's data.
Did a specific campaign or email send drive site activity?
Set the range to cover the send window and look for spikes in the Site Engagement chart (form submissions in orange). Cross-reference with the lead sources chart for Email Marketing volume.

What This Report Does Not Cover (Yet)

Individual page or blog post performance
This report shows site-level aggregates. Page-level reporting (top posts, top landing pages, individual conversion rates) requires a separate view in GA4 or GSC directly.
Blog conversion tracking
Currently in progress (MKB-5428). Top blog posts do not yet have CTA conversion tracking configured. Once blog CTAs are in place, blog-driven conversions will appear in form submissions and new contacts.
Paid advertising performance
Google Ads and AdWerx are not yet connected to HubSpot. Paid Search contacts reflect UTM-tagged visits only, not full ad spend or ROAS data.
Pipeline and revenue influence
The report shows web-to-contact conversion. Connecting those contacts to open opportunities, pipeline stage, and closed revenue requires HubSpot lifecycle stage configuration and Salesforce alignment — a planned FY27 initiative.
