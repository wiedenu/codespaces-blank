The build

Sync high-value marketing activities from HubSpot onto the Salesforce contact record, where they roll up to the account. Two purposes, in priority order:

Primary — durable history + AI-ready data. Weeks later, when a rep, manager, or AI agent opens the account, the marketing engagement is right there in context, and it’s fuel for the Salesforce-side AI we’re heading toward (account summaries, Agentforce).

Secondary, selective — a rep Task, only when a signal earns it. Forms already notify, so this is the narrowest job. Low bar to write to the record; high bar to fire a Task.

What Sales gets: the full marketing timeline on the account, a Task only when it’s hot that reads “software-financing inquiry” not “form fill,” and the which form / which integration carried across so the rep knows the vertical and stack before the call.

The triggers

PHASE 1Launch — hand-raisers
  • Financing-inquiry form submitted (which of 4 forms)Ready

  • Demo / meeting booked (HubSpot Meetings)Confirm
PHASE 2Expand — warming patterns

  • Case-study content consumedReady
  • Product / integration eBook downloadedConfirm

Deliberately excluded (noise): email opens/sends, generic blog views, newsletter subscribe, generic ebooks, list-membership changes, generic clicks/social, unsubscribes (route to ops).

Purpose, trigger set, phasing, and exclusions are decided. These three are the Salesforce-side unknowns I can’t close on my own:

  3. Feasibility of the “Confirm” triggers — which are realistic to fire from HubSpot, and which are more trouble than they’re worth.
