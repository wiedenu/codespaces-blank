Landing Page Value Assessment

Sales Rep & Account Support Pages — Are They Earning Their Keep?
John Wiedenheft, Director of Marketing Operations  |  June 2026
THE QUESTION

Do our rep- and account-specific landing pages justify their upkeep?

  • GreatAmerica maintains 427 published landing pages across HubSpot.
  • A meaningful share are built to support a specific sales rep, manufacturer, or franchise account rather than general lead generation.
  • Each one carries naming, governance, and CMS maintenance overhead.
  • This assessment pulls 90 days of HubSpot analytics to see what they're actually producing.
METHODOLOGY

How the pages were classified

  • Vendor/account pages (67): named after a specific manufacturer or franchise partner — e.g. CNST - Yanmar, SMG - Hunter Engineering, NAPA P3 Sales Tools.
  • Individual rep pages (21): named after a specific person — e.g. "Meet Nate Eades," Field Sales Team Contact Page - Von Ahsen.
  • Excluded (21): generic team/contact sheets (e.g. GPSG Team Sheet, SMG Healthcare Contact Sheet) — these are permanent infrastructure, not part of this evaluation.
  • General marketing (318): everything else — webinars, ebooks, thank-you pages, campaign content.
Data source: HubSpot CMS + Analytics API, 90-day window (Mar 20 – Jun 18, 2026).

RECOMMENDATION

Three different actions for three different problems

  • CSS redirect pages: don't judge these on submission rate — get application volume from whoever owns the credit-app platform, attributed back to the referring page. That's the real conversion event, and we can't see it yet.
  • The four pages with a real, unused form (Palo Alto Networks, Kyocera, ChargePoint, Myers Industries): worth a content/CRO review — the mechanism exists and isn't working.
  • Enablement resources like NAPA P3: pull these out of any lead-gen scorecard entirely. Wrong page, wrong metric.
  • Individual rep pages: keep them, and consider expanding the format to more reps.
CAVEATS

What this is and isn't

This sampled 17 of the 67 vendor pages by checking actual rendered content, not just HubSpot metrics. The pattern was consistent, but a full audit of all 67 would confirm whether any other pages belong in the "real form, not converting" group.
  • We do not currently have visibility into application volume on the credit-app/CSS side — that's the missing piece for judging the redirect-page group fairly.
  • Seasonal or event-driven pages (tradeshow handouts, in-person promos) may show low digital traffic by design, not failure.
  • This is a starting point for a conversation with each segment, not a final retirement list.
