What we’re syncing, and how Sales uses it

Marketing activities synced to the Salesforce contact record, where they roll up to the account. Durable history first, and a rep notification only when it earns one.

From John Wiedenheft To Cody Jandik Re Activity Sync build (parked since 6/26) Date July 10, 2026

Build

We sync high-value marketing activities from HubSpot onto the Salesforce contact record, where they roll up to the account. Reps asked for activity history, but forms already fire HubSpot notifications — so the notification isn’t the gap. “Get activity into Salesforce” is really three jobs, and we’re prioritizing them, not treating them as equal:

  1. Primary — a durable record on the account.The point isn’t the ping in the moment. It’s that weeks later, when a rep, a manager, or an AI agent opens the account, the marketing engagement is right there on the timeline, in context. A system-of-record job, not a notification job.
  2. Primary — AI-ready data, forward-looking.Activity living natively in Salesforce is fuel for the AI tooling we’re moving toward: account summaries, next-best-action, Agentforce / Data 360. Signal stranded in HubSpot can’t feed a Salesforce-side agent.
  3. Secondary, selective — a real-time “look now” nudge.The Task. Genuinely useful, but the narrowest of the three and the easiest to turn into noise — and for forms, we largely have it already. It fires only when a trigger earns it.

What that decides for everything below: a low bar to write an activity to the record (completeness helps account review and the AI) and a high bar to fire a rep Task (protect against noise). Every trigger is already tagged on that basis — record-only vs. record + notify.

What Sales gets out of it. Open any account and the full marketing timeline is there in context, not a ping they missed last month. A Task fires only when it’s hot, and it reads “software-financing inquiry,” not “form fill.” Because the sync carries which form and which integration, the rep walks in already knowing the vertical and the tech stack. And the same structured activity is what feeds account summaries and next-best-action as we move to Agentforce.

The triggers below are grounded in a live audit of greatamerica.com — every one points to a real page, form, or tool we already have. Phases are ordered by rep-notification value; the record/AI value runs underneath all of them. The Confirm w/ you tags mark the handful that need your call before build.

Two-phase rollout. Phase 1 (Launch) is the explicit hand-raisers that are trackable today: turn these on, keep volume low, and prove reps trust the Task before adding more.
