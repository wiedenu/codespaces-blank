GOIZ Analytics — What's Already Available

Prepared for Brenda Stroschein · GreatAmerica Marketing Operations · July 1, 2026
Good news going into today's conversation: GA4 is actively collecting data on GOIZ right now (confirmed via Realtime traffic during setup check). The CSP block that had been holding this up since April (MKB-3913) has cleared — confirmed directly in the page source, which now explicitly allowlists googletagmanager.com and google-analytics.com. Two standard dashboards — no building required — cover most of what's below today.
1

Engagement Overview

GA4 → Reports → Engagement → Overview

One screen answers two of your questions: a table of top pages/titles by views (where traffic concentrates), plus average engagement time per user and per session (how long people stick around).

2

Retention Overview

GA4 → Reports → Retention

New vs. returning users and a retention curve out of the box. Toggle the trend chart to daily granularity and the weekday/weekend cadence is visible at a glance — a good-enough read on "daily vs. weekly" usage without any custom build.

Next step: GA4 access

Confirm who on your team needs direct GA4 access so these two dashboards don't have to route through MOps every time. We can add viewer/analyst access this week.

Going deeper later (optional, not needed today): an hour-of-day heat map is buildable in GA4 Explore, and a true day-of-week breakdown requires piping the data into Looker Studio — GA4's native Explore tool doesn't expose day-of-week as a dimension. Worth a follow-up build if this level of granularity turns out to matter.

Covers: top pages, time on site, visit frequency  |  Not covered here: per-page action tracking, pathways, drop-off, session replay, operation timing — see Sections 2 & 3 of today's discussion.
