MKB-2355 · Phase 2 · Straw Model

GreatAmerica.com KPI Signals

A starting point for the 7/21 Marketing Strategy Meeting — proposed website KPI signals for the group to shape, not a decision.

John Wiedenheft · FY27 (benchmark year) · Built on the Website Measurement Charter

How to read this. One proposed signal for each job the site does, so we have something concrete to react to. It carries no target numbers — FY27 sets baselines; targets come later, once they're trustworthy. React, cut, and swap freely.

Proposed signals — by the site's job, in priority order

1

Generate qualified demand

Lead Generation — #1 priority

Are the right audiences raising their hand with real intent?

Site-sourced SAL trend
Sales-Accepted Leads from website actions, watched directionally. Context: form-fill rate by page type, assisted conversions.

Why: the MQL → SAL shift (Heather with Dusty & Courtney) forces a quality lens over a volume one, and this maps most directly to the enterprise Growth goal.

2

Educate & engage

Prospects, partners, customers

Are people building understanding of what we do — engaging with content, not just landing on it?

Engaged-session trend on cornerstone content
Meaningful engagement on key solution, industry, and value pages. Context: educational → conversion pathing, returning visitors.
3

Reinforce the GreatAmerica Experience

Clarity, low friction · Customer Focus goal

Does the site reduce friction and confusion in how people navigate and understand us?

Friction trend on key paths
A friction proxy — e.g., on-site search "no result" rate + key-path completion. Context: search success rate, exits at friction points.
Two ground rules. Page type before interpretation — a high exit rate is bad on a contact page and fine on a single-answer utility page, so there's no global bounce reading. Vanity guardrail — raw sessions, pageviews, and time stay context, never KPIs; if a number can rise while the site does its job no better, it isn't a KPI.
