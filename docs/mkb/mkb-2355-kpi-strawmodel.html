MKB-2355 · Phase 2 · Straw Model

GreatAmerica.com KPI Signals

A starting point for the Marketing Strategy Meeting — proposed website KPI signals for the group to shape, not a decision.

John Wiedenheft · FY27 (Take Off / benchmark year) · Built on the Website Measurement Charter (approved Apr 2026)

How to read this. This proposes what to measure for each job the site does, so we have something concrete to react to. It deliberately carries no target numbers — FY27 is a benchmark year, and targets get set collectively once baselines are trustworthy. Everything here follows the charter: signals over raw counts, trends over absolutes, and page type before interpretation. React, cut, and swap freely.

Proposed KPI signals — by the site's job, in priority order

1

Generate qualified demand

Lead Generation — #1 priority · ties to FY27 Growth goal

Are the right audiences raising their hand with real intent — and does that intent translate into sales-accepted opportunity?

Proposed primary signal

Site-sourced SAL trend
Sales-Accepted Leads originating from website actions, watched directionally over time.

Supporting / context metrics

  • Form-fill conversion rate by page type (demo, contact, quote, gated content)
  • Assisted conversions — the site's role in multi-touch journeys, not just last-click
  • Lead → SAL progression (quality of what the site hands off)

Why this one: We shifted the scorecard from MQL to SAL (from Heather's conversation with Dusty & Courtney) precisely because it forces a quality lens instead of a volume one. This is the site job that maps most directly to the enterprise Growth goal ($1.767B originations).

2

Educate & engage prospects, partners, and customers

Builds understanding of our value & differentiation

Are people actually building understanding of what we do and why we're different — engaging with the content, not just landing on it?

Proposed primary signal

Engaged-session trend on cornerstone content
Meaningful engagement (depth / engaged sessions) on key solution, industry, and value pages — directional.

Supporting / context metrics

  • Pathing from educational content → conversion pages (does learning lead to raising a hand?)
  • Returning-visitor trend on cornerstone pages
  • Resource / content engagement (downloads, video completion)

Why this one: Educational content is what warms downstream sales conversations. We measure engagement quality, not pageview volume — a page can be popular and still fail this job.

3

Reinforce the GreatAmerica Experience

Clarity, confidence, professionalism · low friction · ties to Customer Focus goal

Does the site reflect clarity and professionalism, and reduce friction and confusion in how people navigate and understand us?

Proposed primary signal

Friction trend on key paths
A friction proxy — e.g., on-site search "no result" rate + key-path completion — watched for direction, not an absolute score.

Supporting / context metrics

  • On-site search success rate (are people finding what they came for?)
  • Page experience / Core Web Vitals on high-traffic entry pages
  • Exits at known friction points in key journeys
  • Voice-of-customer snippets where available (ties to enterprise Customer Focus goal)

Why this one: Experience resists a single number, so we track friction proxies + trend rather than pretend one metric captures it. This is the site-level expression of the FY27 Customer Focus goal — using the voice of the customer to reduce cost and strengthen service.

Two things that hold the whole model together

Page type before interpretation

We define page types first — conversion, educational, navigational/utility — because the same metric means opposite things across them. A high exit rate on a contact page is a problem; on a single-answer utility page it can be success. No global bounce-rate reading.

The vanity-metric guardrail

Raw sessions, pageviews, and average time stay as context only — never KPIs. This is the HR resume-count trap: inflate a numerator without changing an outcome. If a number can go up while the site does its job no better, it isn't a KPI.

On targets. FY27 establishes baselines and watches direction. We set directional targets only once the baselines are trustworthy — likely late FY27 into FY28. Bringing target numbers to this meeting would be guessing; the job right now is agreeing on the signals worth trending.

Open questions for the group to decide

  • Which 1–2 signals per role become the actual FY27 KPIs — and which drop to supporting context?
  • SAL definition: does the site-sourced SAL definition line up with what Dusty & Courtney landed on?
  • Attribution: what counts as "site-sourced" — last-touch, or assisted/multi-touch?
  • Page types in scope: which page types are we committing to instrument and report on this year?
  • Cadence & owner: how often do we review these, and who owns the read-out?
