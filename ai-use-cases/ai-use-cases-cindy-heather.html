AI Use Case: Prospecting New Customers

Marketing Operations  ·  Dealer & Vendor Network Growth  ·  B2B Equipment Financing

The Business Problem

GreatAmerica's growth depends on identifying and onboarding new equipment dealers and vendors — but prospecting is manual, inconsistent, and slow. Sales reps spend hours cross-referencing equipment verticals, dealer lists, and firmographic data to build target lists that are often stale by the time outreach begins. There is no systematic way to prioritize which prospects fit the dealer profile best, so high-potential accounts get the same attention as poor-fit ones.

Version 1 AI Assistant (e.g. Microsoft Copilot)
1
Define your target dealer profile — vertical, geography, company size, revenue range — and paste examples of top-performing dealers You
2
Copilot synthesizes your input into a written ICP document — firmographic, vertical, and behavioral traits of best-fit dealers AI
3
Export a raw prospect list from ZoomInfo, LinkedIn Sales Navigator, or industry association directories using manual filters You
4
Upload CSV to Copilot in Excel — Copilot scores and tiers each prospect against your ICP with plain-English rationale AI
5
Copilot drafts a 3-touch outreach sequence personalized by equipment vertical for Tier 1 prospects AI
6
Manually import into HubSpot, create sequences, assign to reps, and set enrollment triggers You
Version 2 AI Agent (e.g. Claude Code, GitHub Copilot Agent)
1
Set ICP criteria once — verticals, geography, revenue range, exclusion list. Periodic updates only. You
2
Agent queries ZoomInfo, LinkedIn, and industry databases on a schedule — pulls net-new prospects, deduplicates against HubSpot and Salesforce, flags active deals AI
3
Agent scores each prospect against a pre-trained ICP model using firmographics, enrichment data, and GreatAmerica's historical win/loss data from Salesforce AI
4
Agent compiles a one-page account brief for every Tier 1 prospect — company overview, equipment focus, financing competitors, suggested entry point AI
5
Agent writes vertical-specific outreach, creates HubSpot records, and enrolls contacts in the correct sequence — assigned to the right rep, account brief attached as a note AI
6
Review the prioritized rep queue and approve to send — agent logs outcomes back to Salesforce and refines scoring over time You

Why This Matters

6 hrs → 20 min
List build, ICP scoring, and sequence setup collapses from a half-day task to a quick rep review
2–3x ICP fit rate
Systematic scoring against historical win data surfaces better-fit dealers than rep intuition alone
3x pipeline coverage
Reps shift from list-building to relationship-building — agent handles discovery, enrichment, and setup

The Executive Takeaway

With Copilot today, reps work faster but still own every step — the bottleneck is human capacity. With an agent, the bottleneck moves to rep judgment and relationship quality, which is where skilled salespeople actually create value. For GreatAmerica, where dealer network expansion is a direct growth lever, this is one of the highest-ROI applications of agentic AI because the inputs — ICP criteria, historical wins, territory assignments — already exist in your systems.

AI Use Case: A/B Testing of Website Content

Marketing Operations  ·  Website Optimization  ·  Three buyer audiences, one website

The Business Problem

GreatAmerica serves three distinct audiences — equipment dealers, vendors, and end-user businesses — on a single website, making it difficult to know which messaging resonates with whom. Today, A/B testing is slow, resource-intensive, and often skipped entirely because it requires developer time, copywriting, and data analysis. High-traffic pages sit with unvalidated messaging for months, leaving conversion improvements on the table.

Version 1 AI Assistant (e.g. Microsoft Copilot)
1
Choose a page and define the hypothesis — which audience, what the current conversion rate is, what you want to test You
2
Paste current headline and CTA into Copilot — Copilot generates 4–6 audience-specific copy variants in seconds AI
3
Select 2–3 variants and manually configure the A/B test in HubSpot CMS — set traffic split, duration, and conversion goal You
4
Pull GA4 results after the test window and paste raw numbers into Copilot You
5
Copilot identifies the winning variant, flags confidence level, and proposes the next hypothesis based on the data AI
6
Update the live page manually in HubSpot CMS with the winning variant and record the result You
Version 2 AI Agent (e.g. Claude Code, GitHub Copilot Agent)
1
Define the objective in plain language — "Run a CTA A/B test on the vendor landing page. Goal is form submissions. Run for 3 weeks." You
2
Agent reads the live page via HubSpot API, pulls 90 days of GA4 data, and generates a full test plan — 5 variants ranked by predicted impact, traffic split, significance threshold AI
3
Agent deploys variants directly into HubSpot CMS via API — no developer ticket, no manual UI work AI
4
Agent monitors GA4 daily, surfaces early signals, and flags underperformers — you receive a weekly plain-language summary AI
5
Agent declares a winner, writes a one-page result summary, logs to a test registry, and generates the next 3 hypotheses ranked by estimated impact AI
6
Review and approve — agent updates the production page and archives the test. Human approval is the gate for all permanent changes. You

Why This Matters

6 weeks → 1 week
Full test cycle time — enabling 4–5x more tests per quarter on the same team bandwidth
1–2 → 8–12 variants
Per test — AI removes the copywriting bottleneck, enabling true multivariate testing across all three audience segments
0 developer hours
Per test — HubSpot CMS API deployment means marketing runs the full test lifecycle without a single developer ticket

The Executive Takeaway

A/B testing is one of the highest-ROI activities in digital marketing, but most B2B companies run fewer than 10 tests per year because the operational overhead is prohibitive. With three distinct buyer audiences on a single website, the compounding value of faster, more frequent testing is significant. The shift from AI assistant to AI agent doesn't just save time — it changes what is possible, moving from occasional testing to a continuous optimization engine that runs in the background while the team focuses on strategy.

AI Use Case: Lead Generation & Qualification

Marketing Operations  ·  Pipeline Quality  ·  Real-time scoring, enrichment, and routing

The Business Problem

GreatAmerica's marketing team receives leads from multiple sources — website forms, dealer referrals, trade shows, and inbound campaigns — but qualification is manual, inconsistent, and slow. A small MOps-led team cannot efficiently score every inbound lead against ICP criteria before it reaches a sales rep, resulting in wasted rep time on poor-fit leads and delayed follow-up on high-value ones.

Version 1 AI Assistant (e.g. Microsoft Copilot)
1
List your ICP scoring criteria — verticals, company sizes, financing thresholds — and Copilot drafts a structured scoring rubric with weighted fields AI
2
Paste your HubSpot form fields into Copilot — it flags qualification gaps and drafts a revised form field recommendation AI
3
Brief Copilot on 2–3 key ICP segments — it drafts a 3-email nurture sequence per segment tailored to where each is in the buying cycle AI
4
Export a 90-day lead source report from HubSpot and share it — Copilot summarizes top performers and flags where to shift spend You
5
Describe your routing rules — Copilot drafts a HubSpot workflow decision-tree outline for lead assignment by vertical and source AI
6
Implement the workflow manually in HubSpot and monitor MQL-to-SQL conversion rates You
Version 2 AI Agent (e.g. Claude Code, GitHub Copilot Agent)
1
Set ICP criteria and scoring thresholds once — company size, vertical, financing need. Agent applies them to every submission from that point forward. You
2
Agent automatically enriches every new lead via ZoomInfo API — company size, industry, revenue, tech stack — and writes enriched data back to HubSpot. Zero manual lookups. AI
3
Agent runs the full ICP score and assigns a grade (A/B/C/D) — weighted by vertical, company size, form intent signals, and financing history. Updates dynamically as the contact engages. AI
4
Agent routes each qualified lead to the correct rep in HubSpot and Salesforce — by source, vertical, and geography. Dealer-referred leads automatically associate the partner record. AI
5
Agent enrolls Grade A and B leads in the correct segment-specific sequence within minutes of routing — dealer leads get a different track than direct web leads AI
6
Review the weekly agent report — conversion rates by source, grade distribution, routing accuracy, and proposed scoring model updates for your approval You

Why This Matters

Hours → 2 min
Lead scoring, enrichment, and routing — at any volume, from submission to assigned rep
2x MQL-to-SQL rate
Consistent ICP scoring vs. rep intuition — conservative target: 20% to 35–40% conversion within two quarters
0 manual enrichment
Every net-new lead enriched automatically at point of capture via ZoomInfo API — reps have full context before first contact

The Executive Takeaway

For a small marketing team, lead qualification is a bottleneck that doesn't scale — every new campaign or partner channel adds volume the same headcount has to absorb manually. An AI agent removes that constraint entirely: leads are scored, enriched, and routed in real time, at any volume, with consistent ICP logic applied every time. The business impact is not just efficiency — it is pipeline quality. Sales reps spend time on leads worth pursuing, and the feedback loop between marketing and sales becomes data-driven rather than anecdotal.
