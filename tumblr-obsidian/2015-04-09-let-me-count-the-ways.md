# Let me count the ways
**A few weekends ago,** I went to my hometown and fell in love. Mind you, I’m married. I love my spouse, but that’s not who I fell in love with.

> I fell in love with our puppy this weekend. 

**I sat talking with my spouse when I returned home,** struggling mightily to find the words that aptly described what I experienced over the previous 2 ½ days. Because I was listening to a podcast about zen on my drive home, I tried to describe the weekend as zen-like.

> We talked some more, and pretty soon I realized that it wasn’t zen that I experienced. It was love.

**Throughout this heart-to-heart (no pun intended!),** our puppy was cozied up next to me on the couch, snoring to little puppy dreams. As we talked, I realized that our new puppy was her own self. She is not our blessed dog who recently passed away, nor will she ever be like him. She is learning to love us, in much the same way we are learning to love her.

> It’s easy to love a puppy early on because they are _just so darn cute!_

**Other times, it’s hard to love a puppy.** It feels like they are replacing your other dog. They aren’t trained like your previous dog. They don’t behave the same way. All these familiar actions, traits, behaviors, even smells are gone. 

> And the new dog triggers thoughts of self-doubt, betrayal, and sometimes anger. 

**Most of all, what our new dog triggered** for me was simultaneous feelings of sadness and joy. Sadness of the loss of our beloved, and joy at the gaining of new love.

> And that is what I discovered that weekend at home with my parents and our new puppy. I was gaining new love.

_New love is a wonderful thing to behold, and I am glad that I was able to share this new love with my puppy and my spouse._
