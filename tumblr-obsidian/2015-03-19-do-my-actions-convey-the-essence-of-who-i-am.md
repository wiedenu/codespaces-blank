# Do my actions convey the essence of who I am?
I heard this question listening to a podcast several weeks ago. I made a note of this so I could revisit the question at a later point, and only now do I feel like I can at least pose a coherent answer, if not a good one.

Over the past few weeks, I’ve slowly been adding and subtracting values and things from _who I am._ By this, I mean that I’ve slowly been trying to find how what makes me _me_ , and what subtracts from me being _me_. 

Part of finding out _who I am_ [means finding what’s meaningful.](http://journeythroughmydark.tumblr.com/post/113882621216/i-dont-have-all-the-answers-but-i-do-realize)

The other part of this equation is making sure that my actions follow those core values: love, truth, and service.

**Love.**

Do my actions show love? Do my actions convey love? Today, it certainly does not feel like my actions convey love. I am closed off. I am angry. I am depressed. Yesterday, I was full of love. Loving our puppy. Loving my spouse. Loving the world. My anger got the best of me last night and I lost sight of love.

What can I do in my daily life, in my daily struggles, to make sure that love reigns over anger?

**Truth.**

I don’t feel that anger is the _true me._ Even though I’ve traditionally had a quick temper, I don’t feel that anger should or will define who I am - anger is not the true me. However, in moments like these, anger leads away from the truth. It hides the true feelings inside. Anger hides the depression. Anger hides the frustration. Anger hides the love. 

**Service.**

You can’t be in service to others if you are angry. I can’t be in service to my spouse if I am angry. It’s hard to feel generous, to feel like donating, to feel like giving when you are angry.

When you are at peace, you can be of service to others. When you are at peace, there is more of you to give to others.

_Anger only subtracts from who I am. It does nothing to add to who I am._
