# Public versus Private
**I’ve always journaled.**

**To be more specific, I’ve always tried to journal.** Blogs, Evernote, paper, email to myself. I’ve tried everything. Tumblr is only the latest place/tool/hell I use for this journey of self-discovery.

> The question I find asking myself is why do I choose to write my journals privately? 

**Is there an inherent advantage to keeping my journals private?** Is there a reason why I prefer to be private instead of public?

**An example of this is getting my tattoo.** I’m very open on the Internet about getting my tattoo, but I’m not open to friends and family about it. Does this mean I’m ashamed about my tattoo? 

> Quite the contrary, I’m quite proud of it, but I just believe it’s unfinished.* 

**The other reason for privacy** is that I feel my family will place me in a different “group” or “box” in their head; that the tattoo has something to do with my depression (which it might) and judge me.

**I’ve been having dreams lately in which I bring my depression public.** I’m on stage giving a presentation and talking about my depression and what it’s meant for my life. I tell people, I tell the audience that my depression has been a blessing in disguise.

> Depression helps show me what is important in life. It drives me to find a purpose, to find meaning, to exclude those things that take away from my purpose, and to include those things that add to my purpose.

**Right now, this is a tough journey.** It’s an everyday struggle. And not to beat around the bush, this week has been HARD. No energy. No exercise. Not eating well. Not sleeping well. Anxiety through the roof and quite a bit of sadness.

**Not sure if it’s because it’s Friday** , but today is gradually getting better. 

 _Bit by bit, things get better._
