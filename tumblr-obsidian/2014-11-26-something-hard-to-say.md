# Something hard to say...
…but needs to be said.

> A couple of months ago I was diagnosed with depression. 

**What we saw as symptoms of other problems,** were more likely related and caused by my depression. It was a relief at the time, a relief to know that there was something  _actually_ wrong with me and not wrong  _with us_.

**Since that original diagnosis,** however, it’s been difficult. It’s been a road of ups and downs. At the beginning, there were a lot of ups. A lot of “okay, I recognize what’s happening, and it’s okay.” A lot of support from my wife.

**But there was also a lot of questions.** Do I tell my parents? Who else do we tell? Do we tell anyone? How long does this last? Do I have to take medication? How long do I have to take the medication?

> We moved through those questions and more.

**It’s hard to balance those good days and bad days.**

> The good days you think everything is fine.
> 
> The bad days you think nothing could ever be right.

It’s the bad days that hurt the most and continue to color the good days just a little grayer.

**I am thankful for the fact that when I first started therapy,** I saw probably one good day out of two weeks of bad days, whereas now I see one or two bad days out of two weeks of good days.

> A total reversal. 

**I’m hoping this site, both public and private at the same time, will help me and others.**
