# Judging Others
> “Judge not, that ye be not judged.” - Matthew 7:1  
> 

**Anger is a powerful emotion.** Yet, once you dive beneath the surface, there is usually another reason why anger boils to the top. I’ve noticed in my own life that some of my anger comes when I judge other people.

> I judge for many reasons. None good.

**Why are you driving so slow?** Why did you cut me off? Why are you tailing me so close? Didn’t you read my email about this? Why are you asking me a dumb question? Can’t you get off the couch and do this?

> I could go on.

**As I look at these situations,** I realize that I am putting myself first. I realize that, my anger, my judgment towards others, is a result of putting Me at the Center of the world. 

> “And why beholdest thou the mote that is in thy brother’s eye, but considerest not the beam that is in thine own eye?” - Matthew 7:3

**I don’t read the Bible much.** In fact, rarely do I read the Bible. But, as I sat down to write about judgment, the verse at the beginning of this post came into my head. I looked more at the parable in general to try and get a better understanding of my thoughts and emotions surrounding judgment - as this has been a very trying week of not judging others (and failing at it).

**So, as I look at Matthew 7:3,** I come to think of something I read only a couple of days ago speaking to the same point, and that is: “Why is it so easy to look for the smallest fault in others, when we miss our own faults right in front of us?” It’s an incredibly difficult question to answer.

**While I’ve had a trying week, that’s for sure,** I’ve relaxed a bit since two days ago. Not to say that I’m angry, or was, but, oh who am I kidding, I was angry the first couple of days. Last night and today, however, that anger became sadness and disappointment; a feeling of being lost and without direction overcame me the last 24 hours.

**Today, I gradually shifted towards a better attitude;** one where I am still in control of my destiny, no matter what obstacles are in my way. In the heat of the moment, when I was angry, I thought those obstacles were the size of the Great Wall of China. Looking back through the lens of being three days removed from that situation, however, I now see that what I thought was a leap across the Grand Canyon, is merely stepping over a crack in the sidewalk.

**I don’t want to minimize** my feelings nor my anger. I still need to work on overcoming the obstacles in my way, and the emotions will help continue to drive me forward. 

 _Help me learn to not judge. Help me to understand the perspectives and opinions of Others. Most of all, help me be open to the wide variety of experiences life has to teach me._
