# Honesty in the Dark
**I don’t know where to start.**  


**Last night sucked.** Got into a huge argument with my spouse. About nothing in particular. I got overwhelmed by trying to get a lot of tasks done in a short amount of time. My spouse called me out this morning, saying that I let the anger take control.

**Why did the anger take over?**

I did a Google Search for just that question and ended up finding this quote:

> The problem does not arise when we have anger. It arises when anger has us. It’s how we respond to it that determines whether it is helpful to our relationships … or damaging to our relationships … In other words, our response to anger ultimately creates more suffering for us and others, or less. It all hinges on how we process and express it. _(1)_  
> 

I read the rest of the article and found some good tips:

  1. Label it.
  2. Identify the fear fueling it.
  3. Give yourself time out. 
  4. Consciously choose your response. 
  5. Don’t bottle it up. 
  6. Create daily rituals for staying centered. 



**As I read those tips,** I now realize that I’ve gotten away from daily habits I’ve been building the last couple of weeks to create mindfulness and calmness. 

**Death.**

We lost our dog to cancer this week. Our schedules and routines went completely out the window as we filled our time with vet appointments, last meals, and last walks. Not only that, but the cloud of grief and depression hung over both of us this week.

**Lack of Exercise.**

The change in our schedules prevented me, in my mind, from the daily running I had built up the last two weeks.

**Lack of Meditation.**

I’ve started meditating for 10 minutes a day using the [Headscape App](https://www.headspace.com/headspace-meditation-app). (You can see my posts on that [here](http://journeythroughmydark.tumblr.com/post/109319316171/headspace) and [here](http://journeythroughmydark.tumblr.com/post/109728668801/headscape-part-2).) This week, I got away from that.

I’m not saying these three things are excuses for the way I behaved last night. Far from it. Instead, I’m using this as time to reflect on what _I need_ to not let anger own me.

_“The problem does not arise when we have anger. It arises when anger has us.”_
