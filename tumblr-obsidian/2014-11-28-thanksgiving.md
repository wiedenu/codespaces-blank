# Thanksgiving
I know I should be happy.

…

I just seem to be tired.

…

**It’s not that I feel like I’m depressed.** It’s not like I feel like I want to stay in bed all day. Now, I’ve certainly had my fair share of long naps this holiday so far.

> And that in itself is not a bad thing. 

**It’s just that I would rather just sit and read a book,** or be out on a walk, or out running, or just doing anything. I don’t know. One part of therapy that we’ve been working on is communication. The idea that I need to communicate to others what’s going on in my mind so that they can better understand. But it’s difficult.

> It’s difficult to explain to people that sometimes, right now, I just don’t care.

**And now that I think of it,** we’ve talked about that in therapy as well. Maybe instead of saying “I don’t care,” which I normally tend to do, I should say “I prefer the first one.” Do I say that even if I don’t really care, though?

> When do I start telling white lies? 

**Sometimes I worry if I’m ever going to get better.** I think, oh if I just quit my job and start my own business, or if I just make it through Thanksgiving, or if I just make it to the weekend – It will all be better.

**But it’s usually not, is it?**

> It usually gets better when I communicate what I want to do instead of just saying “I don’t care” and going with the flow.

There’s a certain part of my adaptability strength that doesn’t lend itself to helping me and my life.

…

Wow, this has been actually a very interesting time here. Huh.

_There is something to this journaling thing after all._
