# Depression is pain.
**As I travel this journey,** ~~I’ve discovered, no,~~ I’ve been learning more about myself and how to listen to my mind and my body. Where I previously would lash out and cause others pain when I thought I was angry, I now know that when I am angry, I am usually in pain myself.

**The conversation in a recent podcast** turned towards the idea that we might transfer emotional pain into a desire for physical pain. In other words, we want to feel physically what our minds are going through, either to make the pain feel real, or as a replacement for the emotional pain.

> We want to feel our pain, to know it’s real. We want others to tell us that our pain is real, and not make-believe.

**This idea was interesting for a second reason as well** and that is the experience I’ve had with my better half. Before we started therapy, we had many arguments, intense arguments, and I would say and do things that pushed our relationship to the edge. 

> It was a dangerous place for us to live. _(not physically of course, but as a relationship, as a couple - just to make clear)_

**What would tend to calm me down** during those episodes was when I finally got a reaction out of my better half; either yelling back or any reaction really. I would finally come out of my rage and back into the real world.

> I could see that what I was doing was causing pain.

**And so, I wonder, does this idea of transferring our own emotional pain into physical pain carry over to other individuals?** Do we want to transfer our personal pain onto others? I think for me it was unintentional that this transference happened. I do think that it’s important that I’ve seen this and now recognize what was happening.

> We can’t hide from our pain.

**It does us, nor anyone else, any good** to use our pain as a reason to cause others pain. There are plenty of people who want to help with our pain if we let them help us. 

**Today, it’s easier** (not easy) for my better half to help during my episodes. I’ve recognized that they are very intentional in helping during those periods. And it helps.

> I will always experience pain.

**We all do.** It’s a part of being human. How we deal with that pain is also what makes us human.

_Hopefully, building my awareness of how pain affects me and others will only help myself and others over time._
