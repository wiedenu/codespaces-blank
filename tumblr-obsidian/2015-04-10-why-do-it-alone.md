# Why do it alone?
> Why do I write for myself?   
> 

I asked myself this question the other night as I laid in the dark surrounding my bed. 

> Why do I write in public versus writing in a journal?  
> 

**I mean, why not just have a journal next to my bed,** in my work bag, with me at all times? The journal — and the secrets held within — would be private. It would only be for me. And I think, maybe, that is why I don’t want this to be completely private. 

> I want to have at least the possibility that what I write will help not only me, but will help someone else.

**I’ve been asking the above larger questions** about my writing since I started this site. I used to have the feeling I needed to write everyday. If I didn’t write, then I wouldn’t get better. I know now that is not true.

> Getting better is independent of whether I write or not. 

**I don’t believe writing will not help or doesn’t help.** I believe writing does help. I believe writing has helped me focus on what matters in my treatment, in my relationships, in life. 

> The beginning question — Why do I write alone? — remains. 

**Why do I do it without putting my name on it?** Why is my spouse the only one who even knows about this? Not even my therapist knows. Part of me continues to write because I want it to help someone else besides me. Part of me wants someone to read what I’m writing and know that they will be okay.

> Yes, it’s bad. Yes, it might get worse. But, it does get better. It _has_ gotten better.

**I needed to ask for help for it to get better.** I couldn’t have done this alone. I couldn’t have done it without my spouse. I couldn’t have done it without the support of my parents. It wouldn’t have been possible. 

> So why do I continue to write alone? 

 _Because I guess I’m still too scared to let people know. To let people inside._
