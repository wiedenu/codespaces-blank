# Breaking the pattern
**Just finished an appointment with my therapist.** We talked about the fact that for the past month my weekends have, let’s just say, less than stellar. 

A couple points were made by the therapist. 

  1. My weeks were good.
  2. Parts of the weekend were good.
  3. Overall, only a small part of the week/weekend was less than good. 
  4. There’s nothing inherently wrong with wanting to lounge around and do nothing on the weekend. 



**But the biggest point that I’m trying to remember** and hence why I’m writing this immediately after my appointment, is the idea of breaking the pattern of depression. 

> Depression takes advantage of the fact that our brains train themselves to make tasks easier.

**We make it easier** to remember pleasure, make it easier to remember pain, and also make it easier to go down the path of depression. We train ourselves to go down that dark path because it’s what we know, because it’s the easy path to take. 

**When we break the pattern,** when we do something that switches our mind to say, “That’s new. I might think about not going down that path of depression now.” And it’s not necessarily that were doing anything cognizant in that moment. It’s just the idea that we’re doing something novel. 

 _Taking the path less traveled._
