# Being Tired
**What do you do when you’re so tired** that you just want to curl up in the fetal position and go to sleep?

**That’s the quandary I’m facing at this moment.** Is it the fact that I’m not getting the right nutrients in my food? Is it overtraining, my body revolting against all the running I’m doing? Or is it my depression?

> It’s hard for me to tell the difference between what may be real and what is definitely not real.

**It’s hard for me to delineate between** something my body is telling me, versus what my mind wants me to think. It’s like being in the book _One Flew Over the Cuckoo’s Nest,_ where Chief, the narrator, keeps switching perspectives between reality and fantasy. His fantasy world is so real to him that he believes it to be the real world.

> There is a difference between fantasy and reality.

**It’s not that I can’t tell the difference between fantasy and reality** , no, that’s not what I’m alluding. What I’m saying is that there are moments where your body tells you something, and in the micro-seconds before you’re consciously aware of what your body is telling you, your mind takes over and gives you _other_ reasons why you feel the way you feel.

> “You feel tired today because you are depressed.”

**That’s what your mind tells you.** That’s what your mind _wants_ to hear. That’s what _you_ want to hear. It’s easier to handle. Easier to explain. I’m tired because I’m depressed. I’m depressed, so I need to go to sleep. I’m depressed, so I need to eat crap. I’m depressed, so I won’t eat anything. I’m depressed, so I’ll be angry and short with others.

> What do we actually _need_ to hear?

**We need to hear about possibility.** We need to hear that this, this feeling, this hopelessness, is temporary. We need to hear that being tired is o-k. We need to hear that being tired is _not being depressed._ Being depressed is like being an alcoholic: you are depressed, it doesn’t go away. 

> You manage the depression.

**Speaking of alcohol,** I haven’t had a drop in a couple of weeks now. The reason? Certainly not because I’m an alcoholic. No, it’s because I was having extreme tiredness the day, or days, after having only one drink. 

> This is something that hasn’t happened before.

**This is one example** of listening to my body instead of listening to my mind. We are looking for patterns, looking for reasons why we behave a certain way. We don’t want to be depressed. We have the capability of being content. But at the same time, we have the capability of being depressed. 

> Our brain, and our body, will excel at what we train and exercise both to do. 

**Our brain, if exercised to be depressed,** will excel at being depressed. On the flip side, if exercised to be content and satisfied, will excel at those. Our body, if exercised to sit on couches, will excel at sitting on couches. 

> Our body, if exercised to move, to run, to bike, to _be_ , will excel at living.

**This has been a good exercise this morning,** this writing. Training my brain, exercising my brain, to think positively, to be aware of what my body is telling me. To be aware of what my mind is telling me.

_To be tired does not mean being depressed. I am tired, and that is all._
