# Miscarriage
There’s a lot to get down. But I don’t know where to start or what to write. So, I’m just going to keep writing.

We just put Nora to bed. Well, Caitlin is still in there, alying with her for a bit. I came back into the bedroom with my laptop and sitting down in the chair, going to try and type for a few minutes.

So where do we start?

We were pregnant. We were going to have another child. We made it to the eight week appointment. My wife saw the heartbeat. We told family. Then almost two weeks later, the bleeding started over the weekend. By Monday we had the news confirmed. There was no more heartbeat. We scheduled ana ppointment to go over our options for later in the week. 

The next day, however, my wife’s body had other plans. She started bleeding as we put our daughter to bed. She doubled over our duaghter’s dresser, saying it was like her water broke. Feeling it gushing, flowing down her leg. And nothing she could do. I got her down the hallway to our bathroom, to the toilet, and she stayed there. 

I tried to keep our daughter calm and in bed. Told her to pick out a couple of books for me to read to her. I went and checked on my wife, but the blood kept coming. Large clots, or tissue, falling from my wife into the toilet. She wanted to get tin the shower, to see if that would help, so I started the shower. She got in, and it didn’t stop. 

Our daughter knocked on the bathroom door. “Dadda, read me.” It’s okay honey, I’ll be right there. Go back to your room. “Dadda, read me.” Okay, I’ll be right there.

My wife by this point is starting to freak out. Not knowing what to do. I’m being pulled between two women who need me. She tells me to call her mom. I call, ask her to help put our daughter to bed while I help my wife, her daughter. She comes 10 minutes later. Goes to check on her daughter, my wife. She asks me to call the on-call nurse.

I call 911. 

Paramedics came about 10 minutes later. Maybe? Not sure. I have screenshots of when I called everyone. To keep a record for the future. To remember what happened. I don’t need to put that here. It’s not important for this part of this rememberance. 

Teh paramedics came. By then, my wife was laying in bed, still bleeding, but she had underwear on, laying on a towel, on the bed. bleeding.

We explained everything to the paramedics. The confirmed miscarriage the previous day. The doubling over in pain, the bleeding, the clots in the toilet – not yet flushed – the bleeding. Not stopping. The ambuelence came. Two more paramedics. Repeat the story to them. 

Wife asks what we’re doing. What’s next? Paremedics said, well, you can either go in your own car, or with us, but you should go to the ER. So, she went with them in the ambulence to the ER. They walked out, helped her out. A stretcher was waiting outside and they rolled her into the ambulence. I never saw this. I saw her on the stretcher. Sitting up. I handed her her phone and glasses. I said I would meet her at the hospital.

I go into my daughter’s room where her grandmother is finishing putting her to sleep. I kiss my daughter, and say that mommy is sick and is going to the doctor to get better. Grandma is going to stay here with you.

I go and pack up some pants, shirts–but as I find out later, no socks, underwear, or a bra–for the hospital. I grab my wife’s purse. I can’t find my wallet. I freak out. Then, I remember I left it in our minivan because the running shorts I was wearing earlier didn’t have pockets, so the wallet was in the cup holder.

Throw everything in my car, back out. Father-in-law arrives. I’m starting to freak out. He heads inside our house. He says thank you. I drive away. Freaking out. Call my parents before I even reach the end of our cul-de-sac. Try to tell them. Tell them. I’m crying. I’m driving. My mom says I am strong. I can do this. Get off the phone, get to the hospital. Take care of your wife.

I get to the hospital. To the ER. My wife hasn’t been checked in yet. I sit down and wait. I text her. I’m here at the ER. They say you havcen’t arrived yet. Wait a few more minutes, stand to wait and talk to the check-in. A nurse comes and gets me. Takes me back to my wife.

She’s on a bed in the ER. Laying in her blood.
