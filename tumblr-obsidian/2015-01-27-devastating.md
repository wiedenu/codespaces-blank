# Devastating
On my commute last night, a guest on the podcast I was listening to made a comment that stuck with me.

> His job as a lawyer was so completely devastating for him. 

**I’ve never heard someone describe their job/career as devastating.** It’s just not something you hear everyday. I guess what I took out of that comment was the question to myself was “What is devastating in my life?" I ask this because the podcast guest literally had his body revolt against his job.

> Is my depression a revolt against the life I’m living?

**In a way it is, if you think about it.** The main "cures” that people talk about for depression are exercise, better food, better company, and plain just taking care of yourself. (never mind the medicine and therapy, as well)

> But when you think about it, it’s all about becoming more aware of who you are, what type of life you’re living, who you surround yourself with, how you react to life in general.

**It’s actually fun to learn to be more mindful** and aware of how I’m reacting to different circumstances. It’s a daily issue. It’s not something you can work on one day and not work on it for the rest of the week.

_It’s a way of learning to live to the fullest, to be content, aware._
