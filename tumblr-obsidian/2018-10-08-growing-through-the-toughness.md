# Growing through the toughness
**I didn’t have a title for the better part of an entire week,** as this was more just collecting my thoughts, letting them evolve over time, and reflecting even more on those thoughts and these writings. I thought about the theme of my past week, about my musings below, and I felt that the idea of “growing through the toughness” embodied my experiences.

**Note, this is not “growing through toughness.”** This is not meant to be writing in support of “grin and bear” or just working through the pain. As my meditation class this morning showed me, pain is a tool to learn. The pain our body goes through is teaching us something about our body, about a position we’re in, or something that’s not “right.” 

> When we have pain in our lives, we can’t ignore it. We must listen to it. We must grow. We must use the pain and this tough period to learn from our experiences.

**The past week has been a tough week at work.** At the beginning, I was very frustrated with my manager/boss. Frustrated with his lack of leadership, mentorship, decision-making. And my frustration is, and has been, showing through. It’s breaking through.

**By the end of the week,** however, I ended up frustrated and in a different position. I was empathizing with my boss. I understood his frustrations because I was experiencing those same frustrations. And while I don’t think his frustrations have changed over the last week, how I’ve interpreted those frustrations has changed.

**And it’s funny,** because when I was first writing this post, I literally wrote the following:

> _I look at my wife. I look at some of my coworkers. All of whom have the same frustrations, or similar frustrations in their own ways and in their own lives. We all do. And I look at how they handle these setbacks, these obstacles, these frustrations, and I ask myself, “Why can’t I be strong like they are?”_ Am I not strong like them? Do I see my frustrations as weakness?

**Or do I use my frustrations as teachers?** Like pain or toughness, how can I learn from these frustrations to grow personally and professionally? And honestly, the answer for that comes from the change I experienced towards the second half of last week. What caused this change? 

**Conversation.**

**I chose to listen to others.** I chose to listen to their pains and frustrations. I wanted to create a open, safe place for conversation. And my walls fell down. Even in the midst of a possibly blood-pressure-raising conversation at work, making the choice in my head to listen and ask questions, lowered my walls, lowered my resistance to others.

I don’t think anymore what I wrote about myself earlier this week:

> **What’s holding me back from being strong?**
> 
> And I just don’t know. I don’t think I even realized what I was feeling until now, until I wrote down that thought. I had that thought a few minutes ago, and it was so powerful that I was drawn to write. I had to write it down.
> 
> What’s holding me back from being strong?
> 
> Why do I have a hard time being resilient? 
> 
> I’ve let this post marinate for a few days over a weekend. I still have the same unanswered questions as before. I’m still frustrated. Yet, not frustrated?
> 
> What does it say about this situation when I need to see my therapist at 9AM on a Monday, specifically, because of a meeting I was going to have later in the day? 

**I’ve been told many things lately.**

I’ve been told to be more patient.

I’ve been told to quit.

I’ve been told it’s time to move on.

I’ve been told to hope for the best.

I’ve been told to prepare for the worst.

**What I wasn’t told was to be a better listener.** To try and empathize with my coworkers, with those around me. If I had been more open, less standoffish, I maybe could have seen these frustrations in my coworkers as well.

**It’s up to each of us to be open to the life experiences of others,** to try and listen more, and to understand where each of us may be coming from. 

 _Personally, professionally, we never know what another is experiencing at that moment, nor at any moment in another’s past._
