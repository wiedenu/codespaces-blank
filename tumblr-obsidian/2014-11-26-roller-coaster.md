# Roller Coaster
When I’m having a bad day, I can’t function. All my energy, what I have of it, is spent going through the motions. When I think about those bad days, that’s why I would prefer to stay in bed under the blanket; why spend energy pretending to be okay when I can save my energy and be safe in bed?

> I can’t seem to see out of the hole that I’m in.

**Even if the hole is only one inch deep, it feels like 100 hundred miles to me.** Slowly, but surely, I scrape and crawl my way out of that hole. It take more effort to crawl out of that hole than it takes me to run a half-marathon.

> That pain is easy compared to the pain of depression.

**Maybe that’s why I’m attracted to running these days.** I used to love running because it’s so natural. It’s freedom. It’s a basic form of self-expression for me. If I can’t run, I’m not me. I am not myself if I cannot run.

**As I write,** I now wonder if running has become an escape from the pain of depression. Exercise creates endorphines, which is supposed to help with depression.

> _But can we become addicted to running or exercise the same way we become addicted to the safety of bed?_
