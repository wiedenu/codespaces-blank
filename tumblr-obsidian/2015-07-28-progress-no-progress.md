# Progress No Progress
It’s hard to see through the fog sometimes.

The last few days have bene this way. There are moments of clarity, but they come and go. Most of the time, there’s only a feeling of tiredness, a feeling that “I just need to take a break right now, lay down, and close my eyes. Everything will be better.”

And what happens. It does not get better.

Moments filled with crying for no reason. Moments filled with me consciously fighting my mind, my body. Anger and confusion about what is happening. 

Why me?

Why is this happening?

I’m doing everything right. I’m eating well. I’m exercising. I’m getting sleep. I thought these things would make it better. I’m taking my medication every day.

And I still get depressed. And not just for a day, but for over a week now.

I watched tv until 3 am last night. Not because I wanted to, but because I didn’t want to go to sleep. 

I didn’t eat dinner. Not because I wasn’t hungry, but because I didn’t want to eat.

I’m depressed right now.

Today is better than yesterday, but it’s all relative.

The worst part of all of this is the fog. Almost a fog of war. A war with myself. 

Yesterday, there were moments of relative calmness and in these moments I could see what I was doing. I questioned the crying, the anger, the scared self. 

Why was I so scared?

It helps me to write this down. I’ve probably needed to write more in the past few months than I have. In fact, this is the first time I’ve written in quite a while. I’m not sure why I was so scared yesterday or the previous days. I’m not sure why there’s a fog surrounding my thoughts.

What I do know is that I have an incredible spouse, so is supportive and caring. I know that I am overwhelming them right now. On our commute in this morning, they told me that they came down to get me at 3am and they couldn’t fall asleep afterwards until 5.

They are taking care of me.

And I know that I am a burden.

I don’t want to be a burden.

I want to be better, and I thought I was getting better.

I realize now that this is, this depression is a marathon. Fighting, dealing with depression is something that is going to stay with me for a long time.

I wish it would go away. But I realize that it won’t. It’s something that I need to manage. 

I wish I had more to say about this, but I don’t. I need to continue to put one foot in front of the other. One day after the other. And I’ll get there.

I’ll get there.
