# The Struggle.
**A long time ago, in a relationship far, far away…**

**I read an article from Zen Habits** entitled “[On Making It Through Tough Journeys](http://zenhabits.net/troubles/)”. At the time, the only thing I could think was, “Oh my goodness, this is exactly what I/we are going through.” I sent the article to my spouse, exclaiming, “I promise that I’m not the reader who wrote in to the site.” 

> Difficult times can be a test of our souls, and as such can be some of the most instructive times possible. - _Leo Babauta, “Zen Habits”_  
> 

**One of the first ideas I came across** is the idea that happiness is internal; we will not or cannot find happiness outside of us. If we do find happiness outside, it is fleeting. I find this to be true in the tough moments on this journey out of depression. 

> The tough days, like today, are the moments, the times, when I look to others to make me happy. And no matter how much my spouse or others try, it is usually me and only me, who brings happiness back. 

**I can feel some of the tension leaving** my body right now even by writing these ideas down.

**Another idea, the idea that we should embrace** the _entire range of experiences —_ the good, the bad, the ugly — is hugely relevant in my life. It’s something that I’ve started to learn in my meditation practice. 

> Mindfulness is a powerful tool and I’m learning to use it to be more mindful and aware of those moments that are good _and_ bad. 

**The idea in mindful meditation to do a body scan,** to scan from head to toe, and sense different bodily sensations is the same idea as embracing our experiences. A body scan is not meant to fix those parts of our body that are tense or hurting, nor is it meant to embrace the parts that feel good or relaxed. A body scan, in my mind, is meant only to tell yourself where you stand - to be aware of where your body is at that moment in time.

> Or we can embrace the entire range of our experiences. That will include all our emotions, all our joyful moments and painful ones and everything in between. Life is not just the warm and beautiful. It’s all of it. - _Leo Babauta, “Zen Habits”_  
>
