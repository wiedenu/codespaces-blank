# Authentic
**What is the most authentic version of myself?**

A very interesting question that I can’t stop thinking about after hearing that question during my commute. I couldn’t think during the drive what would be the most authentic version of myself.

> Is it during a time when I was younger, or is it something that I’m moving towards and will reach in the future (near or far)?

**What questions do I ask myself to determine my authenticity?** If you Google “determine the most authentic version of myself” you get a bunch of results from life coaches, Dr. Phil, and others. Seems to be quite the buzzword these days. 

Can I move beyond the buzzword and be authentic?

If I think further about being authentic, I have to ask what  _it_ is.

> ## au·then·tic 
> 
> _adjective_ \ə-ˈthen-tik, ȯ-\

> : real or genuine : not copied or false
> 
> : true and accurate
> 
> : made to be or look just like an original

**What does it mean for me to be real and genuine?** How do I make sure I am not being false, or untrue to myself and others? I think I’m on the right path with where my therapy has been taking me. I think part of the therapy is to help me understand what is, no, help me understand to be comfortable with who I am. 

**Ah, maybe that’s it.**

> Maybe what it means to be authentic is the idea that we don’t live by other people’s expectations, we don’t live to please other people’s ideas of who  _we should be or how we should act._

**Now, this doesn’t mean we can go around pissing everyone off.** It means that we need, that I need, to be the best version of myself. When I’m the best version of myself, that’s when I’m the most authentic version of myself.
