# Notes on Authenticity
> Authenticity is correlated with many aspects of psychological well-being, including vitality, self-esteem, and coping skills. Acting in accordance with one’s core self—a trait called self-determination—is ranked by some experts as one of three basic psychological needs, along with competence and a sense of relatedness.

And this –>

> “It’s some kind of epidemic right now,” says Stephen Cope, author of  _Yoga and the Quest for the True Self_. “People feel profoundly like they’re not living from who they really are, their authentic self, their deepest possibility in the world. The result is a sense of near-desperation.” 

_(Source:Psychology Today)_

**It’s funny, this idea of authenticity.** It’s like the scene in the first  _Matrix_ movie where Keanu first sees himself through his “mind’s eye”, or how his mind sees himself. As long as we’re working towards this idea of authenticity, we can build up our confidence in who we are as an individual.

> Part of being authentic, to me, is living up not to the expectations of others, but to live up to  _your_ expectations.

**Our world is one of comparisons,** where we compare our Facebook lives to our friend’s Facebook lives. A world where a Pinterest board on an ideal house (or body, business, yard, pet, kids, family, etc) determines our self-worth, determines our identity. 

> We are no longer who  _we are_ , we are who others say we are; our worth is determined by the public perception of who we want to be.

**If we think about this further, who are the people that we’re attracted to be around?** Who do we want to surround ourselves with? We want to be around confident, authentic people; people who know who  _they are_ , regardless of what others might perceive about them.

> It’s not a question of being an introvert or an extrovert, but a question of we’re confident in  _who we are_.

_Just knowing that’s the goal, to be confident in who I am, gives me confidence. Gotta love those self-fulfilling prophecies!_
